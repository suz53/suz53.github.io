About

I am a member of both Computer Systems Lab (CSL) and High Performance Computing Lab (HPCL) at Penn State, where work with my advisors Dr. Mahmut T. Kandemir, Dr. Anand Sivasubramaniam and Dr. Chita R. Das. My research interests lie in the area of both architectures and systems of mobile platforms. More specifically, I am interested in performance and energy-efficient designs on Internet of Things (IoT) and handheld systems.

Research Experience

  • Optimizing Dynamic parallelism for irregular applications on GPGPUs.
    • Designed a runtime control system which dynamically decide the child kernel launch. Such a control system allows a better mix of dynamically generated child kernels and parent kernels for the scheduler to effectively hide the launch overheads and improve the utilization of the GPU resources.
    • Analyzed data reuse and designed locality-aware scheduler for dynamic parallelism.
  • Compiler-assisted optimization on manycore platforms.
    • Proposed a loop iteration scheduling strategy which considers both bank-level parallelism (from an inter-core perspective) and bank reuse (from an intra-core perspective) for irregular applications.
    • Designed a compiler algorithm that partitions the computations in a given loop nest into subcomputations and schedules the resulting subcomputations on manycore system with the goal of reducing the distance-to-data on the on-chip network.
